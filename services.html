What I actually do

Four things. All of them, I'm in too.

Land, houses, the farm in Ogun, and short-lets in Lagos. That's the whole list. If you're not sure which one is for you, scroll — or just message me and we'll talk it through.

📐
01 — Land

Land in Lagos and Ibadan.

Land is where Nigerians build wealth — and also where Nigerians lose money the fastest. The difference is the title. If a plot doesn't have clean documents, it doesn't make my list. I check before I show you.

What you get from me:

  • The title pulled and read. C of O, Governor's Consent, Deed of Assignment, Excision & Gazette — I know which one applies and how to confirm it's real.
  • The site walked. I check for flooding, access roads, what's coming next door, and whether any government project (BRT, rail, road) is going to touch the land.
  • A payment plan if you need one. Most of my estates run 6 to 24-month installments. Especially helpful if you're paying from abroad.
  • One plot or twenty. Buying for yourself, your mother, or for a build-and-resell? I source both ways.

People who usually buy land through me: first-time buyers in their late twenties, diaspora investors, families pooling for an inheritance plot, and developers acquiring for resale.

02 — Houses

Buying or selling a home.

This is one of the biggest decisions you'll ever make. The way I do it: you stay calm, I do the running around. You only visit a place when it's actually worth visiting.

If you're buying:

  • I shortlist. I inspect. I take videos for you if you're not in the country.
  • I negotiate the price — quietly, without pretending to be more interested than you are.
  • The lawyer, the deed, the title perfection — I sit through all of that with you.
  • After handover, I help with utilities, moving, and a property manager if you need one.

If you're selling:

  • I price honestly. If you're overshooting, I'll tell you.
  • I move it through my buyer network first before it ever touches a public listing.
  • I stage and present so the property shows well.

Who I usually do this for: families buying their first house, people upgrading to a better estate, owners trying to sell cleanly without losing months, and diaspora buyers picking up rentals.

03 — The farm in Ogun

Alabata. Real land. Real harvests.

Most "farm investments" you've been pitched are dressed-up Ponzi schemes — you give money, you cross your fingers. This isn't that. The plots I represent are real land you own, registered in your name, with crops growing on top of it that pay you back every harvest.

I'm growing on plots right next to the ones I sell you. That's the whole reason I'm comfortable putting my name on this.

How it actually works:

  • You buy a plot in Alabata, Odede LGA. The land is yours. Registered. Full ownership.
  • You pay a one-time setup fee — that covers land prep, planting, fencing, drip irrigation, and the first cycle of inputs.
  • You earn from every harvest. Short-cycle crops mean multiple harvests a year, not waiting five years for a tree.
  • The farm runs itself — the developer handles labor, planting, harvest, and sales. You get updates and your share.
  • The land keeps appreciating while it earns. Even when the harvests stop, you still own the dirt.

What we grow:

🌶️ Pepper · 🍅 Tomato · 🥒 Cucumber · 🍠 Sweet Potato

The crops Nigerians eat every day. Short cycles. Steady demand. Prices that have only gone in one direction.

04 — Short-lets in Lagos

A clean place to land when you fly in.

Don't gamble on a random Airbnb. I keep a list of vetted apartments in Lekki, Ikoyi, and Abijo — hosts I know by name, places I've actually walked through. Whether you're in for a wedding, a board meeting, or just a few weeks at home, I'll get you somewhere that opens its door cleanly when you arrive.

What you get:

  • An apartment I've been inside. Lekki, Ikoyi, Abijo — every unit personally checked.
  • A host who picks up the phone. No fake listings. No "the apartment is no longer available" message at 11pm the night before.
  • The price you were quoted. No mystery cleaning fee on arrival.
  • Me, on WhatsApp. If anything goes wrong — power, plumbing, the gate — message me and I'll handle it.

Who usually books through me: diaspora Nigerians flying in for weddings or December, business travelers in for a week or two, and people who just want to upgrade their hotel game without the airbnb roulette.
